The perpendicular distance from a point $(x_0, y_0, z_0)$ to the plane $Ax+By+Cz+D=0$ is given by $P = \\frac{|Ax_0+By_0+Cz_0+D|}{\\sqrt{A^2+B^2+C^2}}$. \\nFor the plane $3x-6y+2z+11=0$, $\\sqrt{A^2+B^2+C^2} = \\sqrt{3^2+(-6)^2+2^2} = \\sqrt{9+36+4} = \\sqrt{49} = 7$. \\nFor $P_1$ from $(2, 3, 4)$: \/n$$P_1 = \\frac{|3(2)-6(3)+2(4)+11|}{7} = \\frac{|6-18+8+11|}{7} = \\frac{|7|}{7} = 1$$ \\nFor $P_2$ from $(1, 1, 4)$: \\n$$P_2 = \\frac{|3(1)-6(1)+2(4)+11|}{7} = \\frac{|3-6+8+11|}{7} = \\frac{|16|}{7} = \\frac{16}{7}$$ \\nThe quadratic equation whose roots are $\\alpha$ and $\\beta$ is $P^2 - (\\alpha+\\beta)P + \\alpha\\beta = 0$. \\nSum of roots: $P_1 + P_2 = 1 + \\frac{16}{7} = \\frac{23}{7}$. \\nProduct of roots: $P_1 P_2 = 1 \\cdot \\frac{16}{7} = \\frac{16}{7}$. \\nEquation: $$P^2 - \\frac{23}{7}P + \\frac{16}{7} = 0$$ \\nMultiply by 7: $$7P^2 - 23P + 16 = 0$$.
